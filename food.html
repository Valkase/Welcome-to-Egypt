Falafel are deep-fried balls made from mashed chickpeas, fava beans, herbs,
and spices. They are often served in a pita bread with tahini sauce, hummus,
and vegetables. Taameya (Falafel) Egyptian food

Mahshi is a dish of stuffed vegetables, such as tomatoes, peppers,
or eggplants, that are filled with a mixture of rice, meat, and spices.

Koshari is Egypt's national dish and a widely popular street food. It is a traditional Egyptian staple, mixing pasta,
Egyptian fried rice, vermicelli and brown lentils, and topped with a zesty tomato sauce,
garlic vinegar and garnished with chickpeas and crispy fried onions. It is often served with sprinklings of garlic juice;
garlic vinegar and hot sauce are optional.
